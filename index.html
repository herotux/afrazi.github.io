Welcome to my website! I am Mohammad Afrazi, a Ph.D. researcher in Mechanical Engineering at Duke University, driven by an unyielding passion for the transformative power of robotics. Beyond the algorithms and engineering, I am deeply committed to creating intelligent systems that enhance human quality of life, with a specific focus on Medical Robotics. My goal is to bridge the gap between complex theoretical concepts and life-saving real-world applications.

With a B.Sc. in Civil Engineering from Shiraz University (2014), an M.Sc. in Civil Engineering from Tarbiat Modares University (2018), and an M.S. in Mechanical Engineering from New Mexico Institute of Mining and Technology (2022-2025), I have developed a strong foundation in engineering principles and advanced research techniques. My work integrates machine learning, artificial intelligence, and non-contact sensing methods to optimize structural and mobility monitoring systems.

I have +4 years of experience in leveraging AI and ML frameworks to enhance smart sensing technologies. My skills in data analysis, project management, and evidence-driven decision-making empowers me to create innovative solutions for real-world problems in smart cities and structural health monitoring.
